# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People in Durham deciding where to get coffee in the next half hour: students,
locals, visitors who just walked past the green shopfront on Old Elvet. They want
three answers fast — is it good, is it open, where is it. Secondary: this is a
portfolio demo showing what a carefully-made small-business site looks like.

## Product Purpose

A single page that does what the shopfront does: makes you want to walk in.
Real hours, real menu, real reviews, real photographs. Success = you can decide
to visit in under thirty seconds, and the page feels like the shop.

## Brand Personality

Warm, plain-spoken, proud of the craft. Family-run and unhurried. British —
"proper flat whites", "homemade cakes", never "artisanal coffee experiences".
Three words: **honest, cosy, exact.**

## Anti-references

- AI slop: identical card grids, tracked-caps eyebrow above every section,
  hero-metric templates, gradient text, buzzwords ("elevate", "curated", "crafted
  experiences"), words for the sake of words.
- Generic SaaS-cream landing pages and editorial-magazine affectation.
- Squarespace café templates: sliders, parallax stock lattes, Instagram walls.

## Design Principles

1. **The page is a visit.** Outside (green) → inside (wood and cream) → sit down
   (reviews) → leave with the details (hours, map). Structure encodes the narrative.
2. **Observed, not invented.** Sampled paint colours, the shop's own letterforms,
   their photos, their reviews, their hours. If we didn't see it, it isn't on the page.
3. **The signage does the talking.** One named type system — the "glass decal"
   (letterspaced condensed caps + thin rule) — used like the shop uses it: on the
   window, not on everything.
4. **Two surfaces and wood.** Green field and porcelain canvas carry the page;
   mahogany appears when you step inside; red only in coaster-sized doses.
5. **Delight whispers.** Easter eggs the size of a dog bowl by the door — found,
   not announced. Reduced-motion always respected.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: body contrast ≥4.5:1 (verified, not assumed), keyboard reachable,
visible focus, semantic landmarks, alt text written in the brand voice,
`prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for every animation.
